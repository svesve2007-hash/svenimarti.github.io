Business Analytics: Singapore Birth Rate Analysis

A data analysis project investigating psychological and financial stress factors affecting Singapore's declining birth rate, applying descriptive, diagnostic, and comparative analytics techniques.

Project Scope & Research Focus

Scenario

Our team decided to analyze Singapore's declining birth rate as a simulated consultancy project for the National Population and Talent Division. Each team member focused on different socio-economic factors, with my specialization being psychological and financial stress impacts.

My Research Hypothesis

Primary Hypothesis: Psychological and financial stress decreases willingness to have children among Singapore residents.

Research Question: To what extent do different types of stress (financial, work, relationship) influence fertility decisions?

Project Goals & Challenges

What were the main goals, challenges & solutions?

Goals:

  • Comprehensive Analysis: Investigate Singapore's declining birth rate using multiple business analytics frameworks
  • Methodological Application: Apply descriptive, diagnostic, and comparative analytics techniques to fertility data
  • Stress Factor Investigation: Research psychological and financial stress impacts on family planning decisions
  • Data Visualization: Create interactive Tableau dashboards to communicate complex socio-economic relationships
  • Team Synthesis: Integrate individual research findings into cohesive, evidence-based recommendations
  • Academic Delivery: Present professional analysis meeting course standards within 12-week timeline

Challenges & Solutions:

  • Data Integration: Handling diverse data formats from multiple research sources - solved by creating standardized data collection templates and cleaning protocols
  • Analysis Alignment: Establishing consistent methodologies across individual analyses - addressed through shared analytical frameworks and regular methodology reviews
  • Time Management: Balancing independent work with collaborative team requirements - managed with clear deadlines and structured weekly checkpoint meetings
  • Dashboard Consistency: Coordinating individual visualizations into a unified interface - achieved through shared color schemes and standardized design guidelines
  • Story Cohesion: Integrating separate analytical narratives into a single compelling story - accomplished through multiple synthesis sessions and narrative alignment workshops

My Role & Analytical Methodology

Role in the Team

  • Researching stress-related datasets and academic literature
  • Cleaning and preparing stress metrics data
  • Developing analytical models for stress-fertility correlation
  • Creating Tableau dashboards for stress factor visualization
  • Synthesizing findings into evidence-based recommendations
  • Contributing to the final group report and presentation

Analytical Framework Applied

Three Types of Analytics Used:

  • Descriptive Analytics: Identifying patterns in stress and fertility data
  • Diagnostic Analytics: Understanding why stress affects fertility decisions
  • Comparative Analytics: Comparing Singapore with Finland (less academic pressure)

Work Process & Timeline

Project Management Approach

Weeks 1-2: Problem Definition & Brainstorming
• Group discussions to identify Singapore's declining birth rate as our research topic
• Brainstormed potential contributing factors and research angles
• Formulated our central problem statement: "What factors contribute to Singapore's declining birth rate?"
Weeks 3-4: Hypothesis Development & Focus Areas
• Developed testable hypotheses about birth rate influencers
• Each team member selected a specific focus area within the broader problem
• I chose to focus on psychological and financial stress factors
• Documented research questions and planned analytical approaches
Weeks 5-6: Individual Data Research & Collection
• Independently researched and gathered data for our respective focus areas
• I sourced stress-related metrics from Singapore government statistics and academic journals
• Cleaned and prepared datasets specific to psychological/financial stress indicators
• Created data dictionaries and documentation for my dataset
Weeks 7-8: Individual Analysis & Chart Development
• Conducted independent statistical analysis on my stress data using Excel
• Built individual charts and visualizations showing stress-fertility correlations
• Created 6 specific visualizations focusing on different stress dimensions
• Prepared initial insights and findings from my individual analysis
Weeks 9-10: Dashboard Integration & Synthesis
• Combined all individual charts and analyses into unified Tableau dashboards
• Integrated my stress analytics with other team members' focus areas
• Created interactive dashboards that told a comprehensive story
• Identified cross-cutting themes and integrated insights across different factors
Weeks 11-12: Recommendations & Final Presentation
• Synthesized findings into evidence-based policy recommendations
• Developed a cohesive narrative connecting all focus areas
• Prepared final report and presentation materials
• Presented our comprehensive analysis to class and instructors

Team Collaboration Process

Our Collaborative Workflow:

  • Weekly Meetings: Regular sync-ups to align progress and share findings
  • Independent Work: Each member responsible for their focus area analysis
  • Integration Sessions: Dedicated meetings to combine individual work
  • Peer Review: Cross-reviewing each other's analysis for quality assurance
  • Iterative Refinement: Multiple rounds of feedback and improvement
