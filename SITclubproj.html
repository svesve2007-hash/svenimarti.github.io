Tote Bag Painting Activity at Daycare Centre

Project Scope & Activity Design

Activity Overview

We organized a 2-hour creative workshop where 30 primary school children (ages 7-9) at a local daycare centre painted their own reusable tote bags. Each child received a plain canvas tote bag, non-toxic fabric paints, brushes, and stencils to create their unique designs.

Project Goals & Challenges

What were the main goals, challenges, and solutions?

Goals

  • Creative Expression: Allow children to express themselves through art
  • Environmental Education: Teach about reducing plastic bag usage
  • Skill Development: Improve fine motor skills and creativity
  • Social Interaction: Encourage teamwork and sharing among children
  • Community Bonding: Strengthen ties between SIT Club and local community

Challenges & Solutions

  • Age Appropriateness: Designed simpler designs for younger children while allowing creativity for older ones
  • Material Safety: Researched and selected non-toxic, washable fabric paints
  • Time Management: Created structured 15-minute activity blocks to maintain engagement
  • Cleanup Concerns: Provided aprons and designated painting areas with protective covers
  • Volunteer Training: Conducted pre-activity workshops on child engagement techniques
  • Weather Contingency: Prepared indoor alternative space in case of rain

My Role: Main Planning Committee Member

Position & Responsibilities

As a member of the Main Planning Committee, I was involved in all stages of the project from conception to execution:

  • Needs Assessment: Surveyed daycare centre to understand children's interests and needs
  • Budget Planning: Helped allocate SIT Club funds for art supplies and materials
  • Activity Design: Contributed to designing age-appropriate painting activities
  • Risk Assessment: Identified and mitigated potential safety concerns
  • Feedback Collection: Gathered responses from children and daycare staff

Key Contributions

  • Innovative Activity Idea: Proposed the tote bag painting concept combining art with environmental education
  • Safety Protocols: Developed child-safe material checklist and supervision guidelines
  • Engagement Strategy: Designed the "Eco-Hero" theme to make environmental lessons fun
  • Resource Optimization: Negotiated bulk discounts with art supply stores

Planning Process & Timeline

Project Timeline

The project followed a 4-week planning cycle from conception to execution:

Week 1: Planning & Proposal
Formed planning committee. Conducted daycare centre assessment. Developed project proposal and budget for SIT Club approval.
Week 2: Logistics & Preparation
Procured materials (tote bags, paints, brushes). Recruited and trained 10 volunteers. Designed activity flow and safety protocols.
Week 3: Coordination & Communication
Finalized schedules with daycare centre. Conducted volunteer briefing sessions. Prepared educational materials about plastic reduction.
Week 4: Execution & Follow-up
Executed the 2-hour activity. Collected feedback from children and staff. Documented outcomes and submitted project report.
